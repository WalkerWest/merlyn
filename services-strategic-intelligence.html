Three engagement modes

Three ways to deploy the L3 layer

The L3 capability is the same in all three modes — twins simulate, agents act, leadership runs scenarios. What changes is who builds it and who owns the operational muscle. L3's DIY path requires the Agent Construction track at L1 — the most technical track in the curriculum.

Mode A · DIY

You build it

After your team finishes the L1 Agent Construction track, they configure twins and agents on the Merlyn platform themselves. Industry templates accelerate the work. Merlyn provides architecture advisory and the platform; your team owns the build and operates the layer.

  • Best for: orgs with ML/agent team + multi-year horizon
  • L1 prerequisite: Agent Construction track
  • Merlyn role: platform + advisory
  • Time to first twin: 3–6 months (after training)
Mode B · Done-for-you

We build it

Merlyn builds the bespoke twin around your specific operational data, decision points, and scenarios. Quarterly executive facilitation included. Your leadership team runs scenarios; we keep the twin current. Most L3 launches start here.

  • Best for: orgs wanting decision-grade output without ML team overhead
  • L1 prerequisite: Business Leaders track (for executive supervision)
  • Merlyn role: bespoke build + ongoing operation + facilitation
  • Time to first twin: 6–12 months
Mode C · Collaborative

We build it together

Merlyn architects code alongside your engineering team. Knowledge transfer is scoped into the engagement — your team graduates to operating the twin themselves. Customers often transition to Mode A (DIY) after 12–24 months as their team's capability matures.

  • Best for: orgs building internal capability + needing decision-grade twins now
  • L1 prerequisite: Agent Construction track in parallel
  • Merlyn role: joint build + capability transfer
  • Time to first twin: 4–8 months

Two ways L3 customers use Merlyn

Concrete examples

Most L3 engagements fit one of two profiles — a mid-market company that needs a templated twin running in days, or a large enterprise that needs a custom twin built around their specific data and decision logic.

Regional manufacturer · capacity planning

A regional manufacturer wants to model how production capacity scales as they add a second shift, commission new equipment, or expand into a second facility. They configure their twin from Merlyn's manufacturing template, plug in their ERP and production data, and run scenarios. Their CFO and COO use it monthly to align on capacity investment decisions. Their team has been through the Agent Construction track at L1, so they own day-to-day operation.

What it costs

Pricing varies by engagement mode. Mode A (DIY) is the platform subscription — your trained team configures twins from templates and operates them. Mode B (Done-for-you) is Merlyn building bespoke twins for you. Mode C (Collaborative) is a joint build with scoped knowledge transfer.

The same Merlyn AI OS underlies all three modes — twins hosted on Dominion infrastructure, same simulation engine, same scenario framework. What changes is who built the specific twin and who operates it day-to-day. Migration between modes is a configuration change, not a re-platforming.

Two SLAs, one relationship. Merlyn's L3 subscription (any mode) includes the application SLA — your twin's data ingestion, simulation accuracy, scenario engine, and (where applicable) executive facilitation are running and current. The infrastructure underneath (GPUs, Kubernetes, storage, network, monitoring) runs on Dominion Hyperscale with its own platform-layer SLA. The two SLAs stack rather than overlap.
