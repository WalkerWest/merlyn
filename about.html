About us

Our story

Why we started this

Every mid-sized American city has manufacturers, healthcare systems, logistics companies, and agricultural businesses trying to navigate AI — and mostly getting it wrong. Not because AI doesn't work for them. Because the people who understand AI at the enterprise level don't live in Evansville. Or Des Moines. Or Louisville. And the consulting firms that do understand it charge rates that assume a Manhattan ZIP code.

The four of us came to this problem from different directions — but three of us came through the same door. Tom founded Sitco, a wireless networking company in Southern Indiana that built and operated broadband infrastructure across the region, ran a co-location data center, acquired a wireless cable company, and had a detailed plan to bury its own fiber before the company was sold. Dan served as CTO at Sitco for eleven years. Todd served as COO. That team already proved it could build real communications infrastructure together — Merlyn is where they finish what they started.

Walker came to Tom's orbit earlier than that — straight out of high school, working at one of Tom's other entrepreneurial ventures while finishing a mathematics degree at the University of Southern Indiana. Twenty-five years later he brings enterprise software, simulation science, and decision intelligence to a founding team that now has the infrastructure credibility to back it up.

We built the thing we wished existed. A locally-rooted AI services firm that works with businesses at every stage — from getting reliable IT in place, to deploying intelligent process automation, to giving executives a real-time financial digital twin of their operation. One that trains local practitioners who stay here. One structured, legally and deliberately, so it cannot extract value from the communities it serves.

Merlyn AI Labs is named for the Vaughan Merlyn / Ernst & Young IT maturity framework — a model that maps business technology capability across three levels and has shaped how our founding team thinks about client engagement from the very beginning. Our technology

Open stack. No lock-in. Reproducible by design.

We made specific technology choices that most AI companies don't. Every component of our infrastructure can be audited, modified, and reproduced — including by every future federation node.

System76 hardware

Servers with open firmware (coreboot) and no hidden management controllers. The full hardware configuration is documented and reproducible — any federation node can replicate the stack from the same playbook.

Why: a federation only scales if every node can stand it up independently.

NVIDIA + AMD Instinct GPUs

NVIDIA powers our production inference and Academy training labs — the industry standard, with full CUDA and TensorRT support. AMD Instinct + ROCm provides a cost-accessible open-source option for federation nodes where hardware economics matter most.

Why: practitioners train on what the industry runs. Federation nodes choose the hardware that fits their budget — the playbook works on both.

Proxmox virtualization

Open-source hyperconverged infrastructure for MSP client VM hosting. No proprietary hypervisor licensing. Strong tenant isolation for the businesses we serve.

Why: infrastructure for community businesses should not carry hypervisor licensing overhead.

NixOS

Declarative, reproducible system configuration. The entire infrastructure stack is defined in code — the same configuration that runs in Evansville can be committed to a repository and deployed identically in any federation node.

Why: the federation playbook is only scalable if the infrastructure is reproducible.

Why Evansville

The right place to build the model

A manufacturing community

Evansville and the surrounding tri-state region has Toyota, Berry Global, Alcoa, and dozens of regional manufacturers whose operational decisions carry real financial consequence. Digital twin consulting for industrial clients starts here because the clients are here.

A workforce ready for the next chapter

The University of Southern Indiana, Ivy Tech, and a manufacturing workforce that has powered this region's economy for generations are ready for AI certification and career development. The Academy trains the talent that stays here.

Industrial heritage, repurposed

We operate in repurposed industrial space — a former R&D center with the electrical infrastructure to support serious GPU compute. A building that helped design industrial products for the American economy now helps design AI systems for the next one.

The founding node of something larger

Evansville is not just a market. It is the place where the playbook for the Community AI Federation is being written. Every client engagement, every Academy cohort, every operational procedure developed here becomes a contribution to a network of nodes that will serve communities across the country.

Our commitment to this community is structural, not aspirational. Merlyn AI Labs is organized as a certified B Corp and Public Benefit Corporation with a steward-ownership agreement. The business cannot be sold to outside investors without community consent. Community shares give local residents a real ownership stake at accessible minimums. Profits above operating needs are reinvested in the platform, the Academy, or the federation.

The four founders earn fair compensation for the work they do. They do not hold any claim to what other communities build with the playbook. That IP, that value, and that equity belongs to the communities that create it.
