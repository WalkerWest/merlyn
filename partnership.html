The partnership

Two companies.
One team.
Designed to work together.

Merlyn AI Labs and Dominion Hyperscale were built by the same founding team and are designed to operate as a system. They are legally separate, serve different audiences, and raise different kinds of capital. But they are not independent — they are two halves of a complete AI infrastructure and services platform.

+

What each company is

Different businesses.
Deliberately designed that way.

The separation is intentional. Dominion's capital structure and Merlyn's B Corp mission require different legal homes. But they were designed to operate as a system from the start.

AI services company & anchor tenant
Merlyn delivers AI services to regional employers.

Three service tiers: L1 managed IT and AI literacy, L2 API-level integration and custom model deployment, and L3 financial and operational digital twins. Revenue comes from MSP contracts, consulting engagements, and SaaS subscriptions.

Merlyn runs on Dominion infrastructure as the anchor tenant — its compute spend provides base-load demand certainty for Dominion's Evansville campus from day one.

Structured as a Certified B Corp under steward ownership. Targets community capital — Reg CF shares at $500–$1,000, USDA financing, and mission-aligned investors.

  • Does not build or own data center infrastructure
  • Does not raise infrastructure capital
  • Does not sell raw GPU compute
Infrastructure developer & operator
Dominion builds and operates the AI data centers.

Dominion identifies sites, raises infrastructure capital, constructs campuses, and operates them. Revenue comes from selling managed compute services — GPU-hours, reserved clusters, dedicated infrastructure — to enterprise clients.

Dominion's structural advantage is $0.02/kWh on-site natural gas generation — a physical asset tied to the site that competitors cannot replicate without the same pipeline access.

Targets institutional capital — infrastructure funds, family offices, sovereign wealth. The business model produces 65–79% EBITDA margins at scale.

  • Does not provide consulting services
  • Does not manage client IT operations
  • Does not run workforce training programs

Where the line is

Who owns what.
Who does what.

The handoffs between the two companies are deliberate and clean. A client relationship that starts with Merlyn can graduate to Dominion without confusion about who is responsible for what.

Merlyn owns and operates

Client relationships — every regional employer, manufacturer, hospital, and university in the Merlyn client base
Service delivery — managed IT, AI consulting engagements, Academy training programs
Platform software — TeamIQ, Academy LMS, SaaS subscriptions
Workforce pipeline — Academy graduates, corporate training cohorts, certifications
Community governance — B Corp structure, steward ownership, community share offering

Dominion owns and operates

The physical facility — land, building, power infrastructure, cooling, networking
The compute cluster — NVIDIA and AMD GPU infrastructure, storage, Kubernetes control plane
Enterprise compute contracts — inference cloud, reserved clusters, dedicated infrastructure tiers
The fiber hub — regional interconnection, USDA ReConnect and NTIA BEAD eligible
Campus operator relationships — Merlyn leases space and compute from Dominion

The pipeline

How Merlyn fills
Dominion's facility.

Merlyn's client relationships are the sales pipeline for Dominion's compute business. Every managed IT client is a future compute client. Every Academy cohort is a future self-service infrastructure user.

1
Merlyn — L1
Managed IT engagement begins
A regional manufacturer, hospital, or logistics company signs an L1 managed IT contract with Merlyn. Merlyn handles IT operations, runs AI literacy training, and assesses AI readiness. Dominion is in the background — the client isn't buying compute yet.
Merlyn revenue
MSP retainer. SaaS subscriptions. Academy training. Non-cyclical — doesn't depend on AI investment trends.
2
Merlyn — L2
First model deployed on Dominion infrastructure
The client is ready to deploy AI. Merlyn designs and builds the model. It runs on Dominion's managed GPU infrastructure — the client doesn't think about the data center; Merlyn manages that. Dominion now has a paying enterprise compute client introduced by Merlyn.
Both companies earn
Merlyn: consulting fees. Dominion: inference cloud or reserved cluster revenue. The client pays both — for different things.
3
Merlyn — L3
Digital twin deployed at scale
For sophisticated clients, Merlyn builds financial and operational digital twins — simulation models running continuously on Dominion's infrastructure. High-utilization, sustained workloads. Exactly the kind of compute that fills a data center and justifies the next phase build.
Sustained demand
Digital twins run 24/7. Not speculative inference — continuous operational workloads that don't go away when the hype cycle turns.
4
Dominion — Direct
Client graduates to self-managed infrastructure
When a client's internal ML team matures, they graduate from Merlyn-managed L2 to a direct Dominion Compute contract. Same infrastructure — different operating model. Merlyn still holds the consulting relationship; Dominion holds the compute contract directly.
Dominion direct revenue
Reserved cluster or dedicated infrastructure contract. Long-term committed revenue that supports the next phase capital raise.
5
Dominion — Enterprise sales
Enterprise sales runs in parallel
Dominion's sales motion doesn't wait for Merlyn introductions. The Dominion sales team makes direct contact with regional enterprises. Merlyn's reference clients give Dominion a warmer door to open — and validate the campus to enterprise buyers who might otherwise wait to see a track record.
Enterprise pipeline
Dedicated infrastructure at $18M/MW/yr. Reserved clusters at $9M/MW/yr equivalent. These contracts trigger the next phase build.

Why it works

Four structural advantages
from the relationship.

Neither company would be as strong without the other. The relationship produces advantages that neither could create independently.

For Merlyn
Infrastructure economics no independent MSP can match.

An independent MSP trying to offer AI infrastructure to regional clients can either rent from AWS at $0.10/kWh equivalent, or build their own facility — a capital commitment well beyond their means.

Merlyn has a third option. At $0.02/kWh, Merlyn can offer clients compute pricing that no independent MSP can compete with. That's a physical asset tied to the site — it compounds with every client Merlyn brings to the campus.

For Dominion
Day-one base-load demand certainty.

A data center with no clients on day one is a liability. Merlyn's managed IT and consulting workloads provide base-load compute demand before a single external enterprise client is signed — making Dominion's break-even utilization reachable from day one.

And because Merlyn's revenue comes from services regional employers need regardless of AI investment trends, that demand doesn't evaporate when the hype cycle turns.

For Merlyn
The same team accountable for both layers.

When Merlyn deploys a digital twin for a regional manufacturer, the model runs on infrastructure built and operated by the same people who designed the service. No vendor relationship to manage, no SLA dispute to escalate.

When a production model fails at 2am, the person who picks up the phone knows both the code and the cluster.

For Dominion
A credible partner that opens enterprise doors.

A new data center company in a secondary market asking a regional hospital to sign a $9M/yr compute contract is a hard sell without reference clients. Merlyn's existing employer relationships — and the live workloads already running on Dominion infrastructure — give the sales team something to point to.

Merlyn converts the campus from a construction project into an operating facility before the first enterprise contract is signed.
