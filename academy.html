What makes it different

Built for practitioners, not slides

Three things set the AI Academy apart from online courses and corporate training programs.

Real GPU hardware

Students train on NVIDIA GPU hardware — the same platform powering production AI across the industry — with AMD Instinct available for open-source and federation infrastructure tracks. No shared cloud instances, no variable availability.

Industry-specific tracks

Five curriculum tracks matched to real industry needs — from infrastructure engineering to digital twin simulation to community node development. You train for your role, not a generic syllabus.

Small cohorts, local delivery

Cohorts of 12–20 students in Evansville, Indiana. Instructor-led by practitioners who use these tools in production. A 4:1 student-to-instructor ratio in the hands-on lab sessions.

Curriculum

Five tracks. One academy.

Each track is a standalone certification pathway. Tracks combine — many enterprise customers enroll multiple teams in different tracks simultaneously. Every Academy enrollee gets a TeamIQ seat for daily practice during the cohort and ongoing reference afterward.

Track 1 · 8 weeks

AI Fundamentals

Baseline AI literacy for every employee. The foundation everyone in the organization needs.

  • What current AI can and can't do
  • Prompting, iteration, and evaluation
  • When to trust an output, when not to
  • AI workflows for daily work
  • Safe, responsible, compliant AI use
  • Enables: L1 baseline literacy across the organization
Track 2 · 6 weeks

AI for Business Leaders

For executives and people managers. How to direct AI work, supervise digital twins, and evaluate AI proposals.

  • Decision frameworks for AI investments
  • Supervising AI work as a non-technical manager
  • Reading and challenging digital twin outputs
  • Governance, risk, and compliance posture
  • Translating AI capability into strategy
  • Enables: L1 executive supervision of L2 and L3 work
Track 3 · 12 weeks

Data Engineering for AI

Technical track. How to build the L2 data layer in-house — API integration, semantic layers, retrieval architectures.

  • API integration patterns for legacy systems
  • Schema design for AI consumption
  • Semantic layers and RAG architectures
  • Data pipeline operations and observability
  • Hands-on labs on Dominion infrastructure
  • Enables: L2 DIY (your team builds the L2 layer)
Track 4 · 12 weeks

ML Engineering for AI

Advanced technical track. Custom model development, training, deployment pipelines, monitoring, retraining.

  • Custom model development and training
  • Model evaluation harnesses
  • Deployment pipelines and serving infrastructure
  • Monitoring, drift detection, retraining cadence
  • MLOps tooling: MLflow, Prefect, Feast, BentoML, Evidently
  • Enables: L2 DIY (custom ML deployment by your team)
Track 5 · 10 weeks

Agent Construction

The most advanced track in the curriculum. How to build L3 — digital twins, multi-step agents, simulation engines.

  • Digital twin construction patterns
  • Multi-step agentic workflows + orchestration
  • Simulation engines + scenario design
  • Operational agents and safety practices
  • Evaluation harnesses for autonomous systems
  • Enables: L3 DIY (your team builds and operates twins + agents)
Capstone · 4 weeks

Hybrid AI capstone

For graduates of Tracks 3 and 5 — a combined capstone where data engineers and agent builders deliver a production system together.

  • End-to-end AI OS implementation
  • Data layer + agent layer integrated build
  • Production deployment on Dominion
  • Team-based real customer project
