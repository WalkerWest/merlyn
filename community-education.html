Four audiences

The community AI pipeline.

Community education is Merlyn's mission-driven layer — separate from the enterprise Academy curriculum. We work with school districts, colleges, trade unions, and civic entrepreneurs to make AI capability accessible at every stage of a person's career.

K–12 schools

Municipal partnership

Computer science curriculum at every grade level — project-based learning for juniors and seniors, and a vocational track covering fiber optics, TCP/IP, and network infrastructure for students headed directly into the workforce. We provide the curriculum, instructors, and GPU lab access at our facility. Districts get a program they couldn't otherwise afford.

Colleges + universities

Rize Education partnership

Small private colleges want to offer AI, data science, and cybersecurity programs — but can't fund the faculty or curriculum development alone. Through our partnership with Rize Education, whose shared course model lets institutions launch high-demand programs at a fraction of traditional cost, Merlyn provides the regional delivery layer: real GPU hardware, practitioner instruction, and local employer connections baked into the curriculum.

Trade unions + apprenticeship programs

Workforce development · roadmap

Data center construction and operations is skilled trades work — fiber splicing, structured cabling, power systems, cooling infrastructure. Trade union members already have the physical foundation. Merlyn trains the next layer: data center buildout, fiber network operations, and the technical skills that turn experienced tradespeople into the builders of AI infrastructure. We're building this pipeline now and welcome union partners to join us early.

Why this is a separate program

Community education is mission, not revenue.

The enterprise Academy is the cohort delivery format for L1 of our service stack — paid B2B training that helps companies build their AI OS. Community education is something different: programs we run because the talent pipeline matters more than the next billable engagement.

K-12 districts can't afford a real CS program on their own — we run them at cost, with the districts paying what they can and Merlyn covering the rest through enterprise revenue. Small private colleges can't fund AI faculty on their own — the Rize Education partnership shares that cost across a network of institutions. Trade unions don't have curriculum for the data-center buildout era — we're building it together because the workforce needs to exist before the data centers do.

The Node Development track is for civic entrepreneurs replicating what we built in Evansville. The economics of community AI infrastructure work better with multiple federated nodes than with a single Evansville facility. We have a vested interest in helping more nodes get built.
